Empowering Embodied Agents with Theory of Mind for Lifelong Collaborative Learning

Abstract

Contemporary embodied agents powered by large language models (LLMs), such as Voy- ager, have shown promising capabilities in indi- vidual learning within open-ended environments like Minecraft. However, when powered by open LLMs, they struggle with basic tasks even after domain-specific fine-tuning. We present MindForge, a generative-agent framework for collaborative lifelong learning through explicit perspective taking. We introduce three key in- novations: (1) a structured theory of mind repre- sentation linking percepts, beliefs, desires, and actions; (2) natural interagent communication; and (3) a multicomponent memory system. In Minecraft experiments, MindForge agents pow- ered by open-weight LLMs significantly outper- form their Voyager counterparts in basic tasks where traditional Voyager fails without GPT-4, collecting 2.3× more unique items and achieving 3× more tech-tree milestones, advancing from basic wood tools to advanced iron equipment. MindForge agents demonstrate sophisticated behaviors, including expert-novice knowledge transfer, collaborative problem solving, and adap- tation to out-of-distribution tasks through accu- mulated collaborative experiences. MindForge advances the democratization of embodied AI de- velopment through open-ended social learning, enabling peer-to-peer knowledge sharing.

Cognitive architecture of MindForge agents. Concretely, MindForge extends Voyager by incorporating three key improvements: (1) **perspective-taking** through a causal ToM template linking agent percepts, beliefs, desires, and actions; (2) a **natural language communication** interface between agents; and (3) **memory updates**, including **semantic memory** of task and environment related knowledge and **episodic memory** of collaboration episodes.

Contributions

Contributions:

  • We propose MindForge, a generative-agent framework that empowers embodied agents with a structured theory of mind representation, natural interagent communication, and a multicomponent memory system for collaborative lifelong learning.
  • We demonstrate that MindForge agents, even when powered by open-weight LLMs, significantly outperform their isolated learning counterparts in Minecraft, collecting more unique items and achieving more tech-tree milestones.
  • We show that MindForge enables agents to exhibit sophisticated social learning behaviors, including expert-novice knowledge transfer and adaptation to out-of-distribution tasks, advancing embodied AI development through peer-to-peer knowledge sharing.

Improving open-weight LLM performance through collaboration

Across different types of open-weight LLMs, MindForge agents coupled with GPT-4 outperformed their Voyager counterparts, collecting more unique items and achieving more tech-tree milestones.
